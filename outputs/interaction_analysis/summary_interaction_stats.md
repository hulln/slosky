## Interpretation bullets (suitable for paper adaptation)

- Across all 141,013 posts, 96,278 (68.3%) are replies, 41,517 (29.4%) are original posts, and 3,218 (2.3%) are non-reply quote posts, confirming that the corpus is strongly conversational in its overall composition.
- Reply share is high in both periods: 54.5% before November 2024 and 68.3% from November 2024 onward, suggesting that the conversational character of the corpus does not depend on the post-surge influx of new authors.
- The median monthly reply share across complete months is 67.1% for all posts and 67.3% for posts by authors with at least 20 posts, showing that conversationality is not an artefact of peripheral one-off contributors.
- Among the 246 authors with at least 20 posts, the median per-author reply rate is 0.66 (IQR 0.38–0.84). Only 9 author(s) (3.7%) have a reply rate of zero.
- 74 of 246 authors (30.1%) have a reply rate above 0.8, and 34 (13.8%) above 0.9, indicating that high conversationality is widespread rather than confined to a few accounts.
- Using descriptive heuristic thresholds (reply rate < 0.2 = broadcast-heavy; > 0.8 = conversational), 74 of 246 active authors (30.1%) fall in the conversational group and account for 38.3% of corpus posts, while 39 author(s) in the broadcast-heavy group account for 5.8% of posts. These groups are descriptive heuristics and should not be read as validated social categories.
- Non-reply quote posts are rare throughout the corpus (below 5% in all complete months), confirming that Bluesky's quote-post mechanism is not a dominant mode of engagement in this language community.
- The final month (2026-04) is partial and is excluded from monthly medians and the subset-share comparison above.

## Suggested paragraph for Section 5.4

The post-type distribution confirms that Slovene Bluesky use is strongly conversational. Of the 141,013 corpus posts, 96,278 (68.3\%) are replies, 41,517 (29.4\%) are original posts, and 3,218 (2.3\%) are non-reply quote posts. This pattern holds across the corpus timeline: reply share is 54.5\% before November 2024 and 68.3\% from November 2024 onward, suggesting that the post-surge influx of new authors did not substantially change the interactional character of the community. At the author level, among the 246 authors with at least 20 posts, the median per-author reply rate is 0.66 (IQR 0.38\textendash0.84), and 74 (30.1\%) have a reply rate above 0.8. Applying descriptive heuristic thresholds (reply rate {$<$}0.2 = broadcast-heavy, {$>$}0.8 = conversational), 74 authors fall in the conversational group and 39 in the broadcast-heavy group; these are heuristic labels rather than validated social categories. Together, these figures indicate that the dominance of reply posts is not an aggregate artefact but reflects the posting behaviour of the majority of active participants.
