## Interpretation bullets (suitable for paper adaptation)

- Hashtag use is sparse: only 6,879 of 141,013 posts (4.9\%) contain at least one hashtag, suggesting that the corpus is not organised around broad hashtag publics.
- Of the top 20 hashtags, 18 have a single author contributing more than 50\% of all uses, indicating that a substantial share of visible hashtag activity reflects repeated self-tagging by a small number of accounts rather than community-wide adoption. The most frequent hashtag, \texttt{#supslovenija}, appears 230 times across 1 unique author(s).
- External link sharing is more common than hashtagging: 17,838 posts (12.6\%) contain at least one external domain.
- The most frequently linked domain is \texttt{media.tenor.com} (2,613 posts, 121 unique authors), indicating that GIF sharing is a prominent form of informal interaction in the corpus.
- By category, GIF/media sharing accounts for 14.5\% of all domain occurrences, Slovene news media for 35.8\%, and platform tooling/Bluesky utilities for 11.2\%. Together these three categories account for the large majority of linked content.
- News-media linking is distributed across several Slovene outlets, suggesting recurring current-affairs engagement rather than concentration on a single source.
- Link-sharing rates differ by post type: original posts link to external content at 32.2\% compared with 4.5\% for replies, consistent with replies serving primarily as conversational turns rather than content-sharing acts.

## Suggested paragraph for Section 5.6

Hashtag use is sparse throughout the corpus: only 6,879 of 141,013 posts (4.9\%) contain at least one hashtag. Of the 20 most frequent hashtags, 18 have a single author contributing more than half of all uses, indicating that much of the visible hashtag activity reflects self-tagging by a small number of accounts rather than community-wide thematic organisation. The most frequent hashtag, \texttt{#supslovenija}, appears 230 times but is used by only 1 unique author(s). External link sharing is more prevalent: 17,838 posts (12.6\%) contain at least one external domain. The single most linked domain is \texttt{media.tenor.com} (2,613 occurrences), reflecting frequent GIF-based informal exchange. Across all linked domains, GIF and media-sharing services account for 14.5\% of domain occurrences and Slovene news outlets for 35.8\%, distributed across several titles. Link-sharing rates differ by post type (32.2\% of original posts vs.\ 4.5\% of replies contain an external link), consistent with replies functioning primarily as conversational turns. Together, these patterns suggest a small, conversational space oriented around informal exchange and recurring news-media engagement, rather than one organised primarily around hashtag-driven topic publics.
