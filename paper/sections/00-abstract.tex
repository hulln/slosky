This paper presents a corpus of 141,013 public Slovene posts by 432 authors on Bluesky, spanning August 2023 to mid-April 2026. The corpus was built through a multi-stage ATProto pipeline: relay-based discovery of Slovene-tagged posts, backfill of surviving public post histories from authors' home Personal Data Servers, and post-level language filtering using langid and langdetect. Manual validation indicates high precision for the included decision groups. As one of the first language-specific empirical studies of Slovenian use on Bluesky, the resulting corpus captures a community in formation: a sharp late-2024 growth surge, a strongly conversational posting pattern that predates the surge and holds across much of the active author base (68.3\% of posts are replies), sparse and largely account-concentrated hashtag use, and link-sharing shaped primarily by Slovene news media, GIF-based informal exchange, and Bluesky-native tooling. Overall, the corpus points to a small public space centred on dialogue and recurring content-sharing rather than broad hashtag publics. The paper documents the collection workflow, reports on its validation, and describes the basic interactional, temporal, and linking patterns of Slovene Bluesky use. It also discusses the legal and ethical questions that accompany corpus construction on decentralised, open-protocol platforms.
